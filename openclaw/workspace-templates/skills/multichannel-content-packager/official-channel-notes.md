# Official Channel Notes

Use these notes when platform-specific behavior affects packaging choices. Prefer these notes over generic social-media folklore.

## LinkedIn

### Organic posts
- Favor professional relevance, clarity, and useful takeaways.
- Strong opening lines matter because the first lines carry disproportionate weight in feed reading behavior.
- Keep copy authentic, useful, and specific rather than hype-heavy.

### Sponsored or paid LinkedIn content
- Keep paid headline-style copy short.
- Prefer value-first framing over abstract branding language.
- Images and video often outperform text-only formats for sponsored content.
- Treat these points as paid-content guidance only; do not force them onto every organic post.

### LinkedIn articles and newsletter editions
- Treat LinkedIn newsletter editions as article-like long-form pieces, not as email newsletters.
- When a cover image is relevant, use a 16:9 note and default to the documented 1920 x 1080 recommendation.
- Keep the article title, cover concept, and opening aligned with the same thesis used in shorter channel variants.

### LinkedIn newsletter management constraints
- LinkedIn documents that members can create newsletters.
- LinkedIn also documents operational constraints for multiple newsletters.
- These constraints matter only when the task is about LinkedIn newsletter operations, not when simply drafting content.

## X

### Standard posts
- Assume a standard post unless the user explicitly says Premium or long-form posting is available.
- Default to concise copy that fits the standard character limit cleanly.
- Use a thread only when the idea genuinely benefits from sequencing.

### Media behavior
- A post may contain text plus media.
- Official help documents up to four media items in a post.
- Video does not count toward the text character limit.
- If suggesting media, keep the note lightweight and compatible with standard posting behavior.

### Premium behavior
- X documents longer posts for Premium subscribers.
- Do not assume that capability unless the user explicitly says it is available for the target account.

## Instagram

### General packaging stance
- Treat Instagram as visually led.
- Do not pretend visuals exist if the user has only provided text.
- Pair any caption with a concept angle and optional asset brief when possible.

### Carousel behavior
- Official help documents one caption and one location for the whole carousel post.
- Official help also documents a conservative maximum of ten photos or videos for a carousel.
- If the user says their account has newer carousel capacity, follow the user; otherwise stay conservative on the documented default.

### Visual dependency
- Mark Instagram `needs review` when the caption depends on missing creative, slide sequencing, or reel treatment.

## Newsletter versus platform newsletter

Distinguish these two cases:

### Email newsletter
- Use subject line, preview text, opening, body, and CTA.
- Optimize for inbox clarity and reader momentum.

### LinkedIn newsletter edition
- Use title, cover image note if relevant, article-style body, and a clear thesis-led opening.
- Do not treat it like an email newsletter pasted into LinkedIn.

## Conservative fallback rule

When a platform feature, length allowance, or media limit is uncertain:
- use the official conservative default
- note any ambiguity in status or notes
- avoid presenting experimental or account-specific features as universal
